The origins and history of modern genetics can be traced back to Gregor Mendel's memoirs on the hybridization of the pea plant (in 1865). By fertilizing pea plants and examining their second and third generations after fertilization, Mendel realized that there are special rules for the transmission of traits. However, the word "genetics" was coined in 1906 to denote the new science and information of heredity. This science, which is based on the Mendelian method for the analysis of heredity, explains concepts such as genes, genotypes and phenotypes. However, Mendel's theories were not supported by scientific associations until after his death; He has three important laws called Mendel's laws, which are:

  1. The law of segregation of genes
  2. The law of independent assimilation of genes
  3. law of dominance
